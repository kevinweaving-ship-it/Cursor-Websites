## Rules

1. **Baseline Regatta:** Regatta 294 (Youth Nationals 2024) serves as the starting point for B fleet rankings.

2. **Eligibility:** Only sailors who:
   - Actually raced in Regatta 294 Optimist B (`raced = TRUE`)
   - Are NOT entered as Optimist A in Regatta 375
   - Are included in the current B fleet list (27 sailors)

3. **Ranking Order:** Initial rankings are based on Regatta 294 results:
   - Rank 1: Harper Madel (Master Score: 1.5)
   - Rank 2: Sebastian Fourie (Master Score: 1.7)
   - Rank 3: Dwayne McCombe (Master Score: 2.7)

4. **Master Score Calculation:** `nett_points_raw / to_count` from Regatta 294

5. **Next Steps:** Additional regattas will be added to build the complete B fleet rankings, with most recent regatta results taking precedence in head-to-head comparisons.

6. **Large Regatta Rule:** When a large regatta (e.g., Regatta 306 with 10+ positions) is processed, sailors in that regatta who have raced against others in older regattas (294, 304) must be positioned ahead/behind those sailors based on their head-to-head results in the previous regatta where they both sailed. However, this rule only applies when sailors have raced in 2 or more of the regattas being compared. If all sailors have only sailed in 1 of the regattas so far, this rule does not apply yet.

## Master Regatta Ranking Logic (Iterative Process)

**Rule:** The **most recent regatta** becomes the **master (main) regatta**. All other sailors who sailed against anyone in the master regatta must be slotted above/below that sailor based on how they placed against them in another regatta (where both sailors sailed).

**Important:** This is a **fluid/iterative standing list** that will change as we check the next/more recent regatta. When a newer regatta is processed, it becomes the new master regatta and the standings are recalculated.

### Positioning Logic

For each control sailor in Regatta 339:
- Their **master position** is fixed based on Regatta 339 results
- Sailors from their other regatta(s) are positioned relative to them:
  - **Above** the control sailor if they beat them in the other regatta
  - **Below** the control sailor if they lost to them in the other regatta
- This creates the overall/master ranking list

### Example

**Liam Geldenhuys** (339 rank 3):
- In Regatta 306, he was rank 4
- Sailors who beat him in 306 (ranks 1-3): Alexa Aab (rank 2), Evan Sterling (rank 3), plus rank 1 (Michael Hartnack - excluded)
- These sailors should be positioned **above** Liam in the master list
- Sailors who lost to him in 306 (ranks 5-10): Should be positioned **below** Liam in the master list

**Result:** The master list combines all regattas, with Regatta 339 positions as the anchor and other regattas used to slot sailors relative to the control sailors.

**Note:** When the next regatta (more recent than 339) is processed, it will become the new master regatta and the standings will be recalculated with that regatta as the anchor point.
